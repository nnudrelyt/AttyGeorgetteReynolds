Wireframe · Process / SEO anchor section
The Process

Here's what
to expect.

Most buyers and sellers have never worked closely with a real estate attorney before. Here's exactly what happens when you do — and why it matters at every stage of your transaction.

01
We Talk It Through

Before anything is signed, we get on the phone. You tell me about your transaction — what you're buying or selling, your timeline, any concerns you already have. I tell you exactly what to expect and what I'll be watching for. No intake form, no waiting room.

02
Contract Review

Once you're under contract, I review every line. I'll explain what the terms mean in plain language, flag anything unusual, and negotiate attorney modifications that protect your interests. In Illinois, buyers and sellers typically have 5 business days — I use every one of them.

03
Due Diligence & Title

I review the title commitment, survey, and inspection response — the documents most people sign without fully reading. If there's a title issue, an unclear easement, or a clause that needs to change, I catch it here, before it becomes your problem after closing.

04
A Clean Closing

By the time you sit down at the closing table, there should be no surprises. I prepare and review all closing documents, walk you through what you're signing, and make sure the numbers match. You leave with the keys — and the confidence that everything was done right.

i

Illinois is an attorney state for real estate closings. Unlike many states, Illinois law requires an attorney to be present at closing — which means you're already paying for legal representation. The question is whether that attorney is working for you, or just facilitating paperwork. [Attorney Name] works exclusively for her clients.

What I Handle

Real estate law,
start to finish.

Every service below is something I handle personally — not delegated to a paralegal or managed through a portal. You'll always know where your transaction stands, and you'll always be able to reach me.

Residential Purchase & Sale

Full legal representation for buyers and sellers in single-family homes, condominiums, townhomes, and multi-unit properties throughout Chicago and the surrounding suburbs. From contract through closing.

Contract Review & Negotiation

Thorough review of purchase contracts, attorney modification letters, rider agreements, and association documents. Clear explanation of every clause — and negotiation of terms that better protect your position.

Legal representation for buyers and sellers of investment properties, including multi-unit buildings and mixed-use properties. Includes review of existing leases, tenant rights considerations, and due diligence support.
