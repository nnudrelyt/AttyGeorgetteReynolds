## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does this look AI-generated? No.** It has a committed POV — warm ivory + forest + brass — carried with real craft: radial-gradient dark bands (not flat fills), film grain, ghost numerals on an ordered set, a faint G-mark watermark, brass hairline ticks. This is "depth, not coverage" actually executed. A visitor asks "who made this?", not "which AI made this?"

**Deterministic scan (4 findings):**
- `side-tab` (line 82): `border-left:3px solid var(--brass)` on `.hero-cred` — the one genuine absolute-ban tell. True positive.
- `layout-transition` (line 157, FAQ `max-height`; line 302, nav `height`): conventional accordion + scroll-condense; minor jank potential, low severity.
- `em-dash-overuse`: **30 em-dashes** in body copy — the strongest AI-cadence tell here, and it dilutes the plain-warm attorney voice.

**Caution, not a ding:** the ivory body bg sits in the warm-neutral "cream AI default" band the skill warns about. Here it's an *existing committed brand identity* carried with craft, so identity-preservation applies — but it's the one thing keeping the palette from feeling fully distinctive. The warmth-by-accent (brass/forest) is what rescues it.

## Overall Impression

A genuinely well-designed, on-brief page: restrained, warm, trustworthy, with copy most law-firm sites never reach. The single biggest problem isn't aesthetic — it's that the **contact form is a non-functional prop**, and on a site whose whole job is *earning trust*, a form that silently eats a worried seller's message is the worst possible failure. Fix the form and replace the placeholder portrait and this jumps into the "Good/ship-it" band.

## What's Working

1. **Committed identity, executed with craft.** Forest gradient bands, brass ticks, grain, ghost numerals, G-mark watermark — restraint *with* intent. It serves the "quiet luxury" brief without going beige-and-timid.
2. **Exceptional copy.** "You're not handed off to a portal or a receptionist; you get her, the whole way through." The FAQ answers are specific, honest, and genuinely useful — they *are* the trust-builder.
3. **Real accessibility foundations.** Skip link, `:focus-visible`, `prefers-reduced-motion`, keyboard-operable FAQ, ARIA regions, alt text, WebP with fallback. Not an afterthought.

## Priority Issues

- **[P1] The contact form does nothing.** There is no `<form>` element; "Send message" is `type="button"` with no handler, no validation, no success/error state. A user who writes a message and clicks gets silence — and never reaches Georgette. On a credibility-first site this actively destroys trust.
  - Fix: wrap in a real `<form>`, wire to an endpoint (Formspree/Netlify Forms/serverless mailto), add required-field validation, inline error messages, and a visible success state. Honor the "I prefer phone" voice in the confirmation.
  - Command: `/impeccable harden`

- **[P1] Placeholder portrait undercuts the entire premise.** The hero headshot is a candid snapshot against a busy red/gold banquet wall — it fights the quiet-luxury aesthetic and is the literal trust anchor on a trust-first page. The code flags it as a pre-launch replace; treating it as a launch blocker.
  - Fix: a professional headshot, neutral or softly contextual background, warm-toned to sit in the palette. One decisive portrait.
  - Command: content/asset swap (not a skill command); `/impeccable polish` once the real asset lands.

- **[P2] Em-dash overuse (30).** The most AI-sounding thing about the page. An attorney's plain-warm voice reads better with varied punctuation — commas, colons, periods, the occasional parenthesis.
  - Command: `/impeccable clarify`

- **[P2] Two consecutive near-identical 2×2 card grids.** "How I Work" and "Where I Can Help" share the same anatomy (brass tick + h3 + body) back-to-back. The italic "angle" line differentiates Services slightly; the stacked sameness still reads as template rhythm.
  - Fix: give one grid a distinct treatment (e.g. a numbered list/row for the four roles, reserving cards for services), or vary column count/scale.
  - Command: `/impeccable layout`

- **[P2] Two smaller a11y/responsive gaps.** (a) Form placeholders are `#A89C86` on ivory (~2.2:1) — below the 4.5:1 placeholder bar (labels exist, so impact is limited). (b) On mobile the nav links (Approach/About/Services/FAQ) are `display:none` with no hamburger or menu — only the Contact button remains; section wayfinding is lost.
  - Command: `/impeccable audit` (contrast) / `/impeccable adapt` (mobile nav)

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (first-timer / anxious client):** Writes a careful message in the form, clicks Send — nothing happens, no confirmation. Assumes it failed or that no one is listening. Likely leaves without calling.

**Casey (distracted mobile user):** Lands on mobile; the only nav affordance is "Contact." Can't jump to FAQ or About without scrolling the whole page. Phone CTA is reachable (good), but the dead form wastes the one-handed effort of typing.

**Riley (stress-tester):** Submits the form empty → nothing. Submits with emoji/long text → nothing. Quickly concludes the form is decorative and the site may be a template. Trust eroded.

**Project persona — "Margaret," anxious first-time seller (from PRODUCT.md):** Everything in the copy reassures her ("calls returned same day," "you get her the whole way through") — then the form she trusts goes nowhere. The page over-promises responsiveness and under-delivers at the one interactive moment.

## Minor Observations

- `.hero-cred` uses a 3px brass `border-left` (absolute-ban side-stripe). Swap for a full hairline border or a brass top-tick to match the card convention.
- Hero headline clamp max is 110px — above the ~96px display ceiling guideline. The `<br>` prevents overflow, but consider 96px.
- FAQ `max-height` and nav `height` transitions animate layout properties; fine in practice, but `grid-template-rows` / `transform` would be smoother.
- Testimonials are placeholders marked "Pending approval" — correctly flagged in code; swap real quotes before launch.
- Full-page headless capture renders below-fold blank because reveals are `opacity:0` until IntersectionObserver fires. Real users are unaffected (it fires on scroll), but it means the reveal default is hidden-not-visible — worth keeping an eye on for any no-JS/SEO edge.

## Questions to Consider

- If the phone is the real CTA, does the form earn its space — or should it be a shorter "request a callback" that's trivially wireable and impossible to leave silent?
- The ivory ground is the one un-distinctive note. Would a slightly deeper warm ground (or the navy/terracotta direction) make the page unmistakably *hers* rather than "a tasteful warm site"?
- The page reassures relentlessly in words. Where could one *visual* moment (the portrait, a signature, a single photograph of Chicago) carry that reassurance instead of more copy?
